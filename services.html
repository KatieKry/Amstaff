Grooming, Health, and Diet

This page will provide you with what you need to know in order to best take care of your Staffy.

Grooming

American Staffordshire Terriers are a short flat coated breed that sheds throughout the year, even in the winter time. Grooming is suggested for 4 – 8-week intervals
to clean ears, clip nails, check anal glands and insure the coat and skin are in healthy condition. Want to know more? We have grooming articles that we can send you!

Health

AmStaffs are affable, smart, and protective of their family. They are a generally healthy breed with an average lifespan of 12 years. AmStaffs can suffer
from common conditions like knee ligament tears and hypothyroidism. Early diagnosis is the key to a long and happy life, so be sure to schedule routine checkups. They typically also have skin and food allergies that may need specific treatment early on. The warning signs of this would be constant scratching, licking, or hives appearing on the skin. In the most sever cases, they will vomit. Want to know more? We have health articles that we can send you!

Diet

American Staffordshire Terrier is a medium-sized, muscular dog that needs calories between 20-30 calories per pound in a day. The amount of food they need to have per day depends on their age and level of activities. The average amount of food an adult American Staffordshire Terrier should have a 3-4 cup in a day. If they are very active and involved in regular physical exercise, increase the amount of food accordingly.Amstaff puppy should be fed 3 small meals a day. They are in a developing and growing stage, so they need proper puppy nutrition. Do not overfeed them as they might gain heavyweight in a small age which might invite many health diseases. So, feed them according to their activity level, age, and size. Want to know more? We have diet articles that we can send you!
